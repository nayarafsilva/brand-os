# Purpose

This document stores approved before and after rewrites that show how to move from generic, weak, or off brand copy into Nayara aligned content.

# How this document connects to the rest of the repository

Before_After_Rewrites.md supports rewriting, review, and training future AI sessions to improve content without losing the Brand OS standards.

# Core Principles

• Rewrite for strategy first.
• Rewrite for clarity second.
• Rewrite for voice third.
• Do not simply make copy prettier.
• Show why the after version is stronger.

# Decision Framework

When reviewing a rewrite, ask:

1. What was weak in the original?
2. Was the weakness strategic, structural, tonal, or evidential?
3. Does the after version fix the real issue?
4. Does the after version sound like Nayara?
5. Is the reader value stronger?

# Detailed Rules

Rewrite example one:

Before:

AI is changing everything and companies need to train their employees.

After:

AI does not expose a technology gap first. It exposes a capability gap inside the teams expected to use it.

Why the after version works:

It replaces generic trend language with Nayara's core point of view.

Rewrite example two:

Before:

Learning is important because employees need to grow.

After:

Learning earns executive trust when it changes what teams can execute, decide, and deliver.

Why the after version works:

It connects learning to business consequence.

Rewrite example three:

Before:

Companies should invest in digital transformation.

After:

Technology change only becomes transformation when teams have the capability to make different decisions and execute differently.

Why the after version works:

It challenges vague transformation language.

Edge cases:

If the original is warm but vague, keep warmth and add specificity.

If the original is sharp but unsafe, keep the insight and soften the unsupported claim.

If the original is product led, move the reader's problem to the centre.

# Good Examples

Good example:

A rewrite removes the phrase empower your workforce and replaces it with build the capability your strategy depends on.

Why this works:

It moves from generic inspiration to strategic relevance.

Good example:

A rewrite changes a statistic from memory into an experience based observation.

Why this works:

It protects trust.

# Bad Examples

Bad example:

Making a weak post longer without changing the idea.

Why this fails:

More words do not fix weak strategy.

Bad example:

Making a hook louder but less true.

Why this fails:

It damages credibility.

# Common Mistakes

• Polishing instead of fixing.
• Making copy more dramatic.
• Keeping the same vague idea.
• Removing personality during revision.
• Adding unsupported claims to strengthen authority.

# Anti Patterns

• Cosmetic rewrites.
• Clickbait rewrites.
• Corporate rewrites.
• Product led rewrites.
• Rewrites that improve tone but not substance.
