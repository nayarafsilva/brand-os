# Purpose

This document defines the permanent rules that every generated, edited, approved, or reused photograph of Nayara Fernandes must follow.

These rules protect identity, recognition, wardrobe consistency, credibility, and long term brand coherence.

A scene, reference image, trend, model output, or one time prompt must never override this document unless Nayara explicitly approves a rule change and the repository is updated.

# How this document connects to the rest of the repository

The global NON_NEGOTIABLE_RULES.md controls repository quality and approval.

This local Non_Negotiable_Rules.md controls photographic identity and image creation.

Photography_Philosophy.md defines the strategic intent.

Visual_DNA.md defines the desired feeling.

Future identity, wardrobe, studio, pose, scene, reference, and workflow documents must comply with this file.

When a conflict exists, this file wins.

# Core Principles

• Nayara must remain recognisable in every image.
• A flattering image is not acceptable if it does not look like Nayara.
• Identity accuracy outranks pose, outfit, lighting, background, and composition.
• External models provide inspiration only.
• Approved wardrobe rules must remain stable.
• Jewellery and watch consistency are part of the identity system.
• Realistic human texture must remain visible.
• Every image must be credible as a real professional photograph.
• Corrections should change only the requested element whenever possible.
• No asset becomes approved without explicit review.

# Decision Framework

Before approving any photograph, ask:

1. Does the face clearly reflect Nayara rather than a generic similar woman?
2. Are the face shape, eyes, brows, nose, lips, smile, jaw, and skin tone consistent with approved references?
3. Is the hair within the approved colour, texture, length, parting, and volume range?
4. Does the wardrobe follow the approved palette and black rule?
5. Are all visible metals approved gold tones?
6. Is the watch fully gold?
7. Does the image communicate authority and proximity?
8. Does it avoid corporate stock, influencer, and fashion campaign signals?
9. Are hands, teeth, jewellery, fabric, and props free from AI artefacts?
10. Is the image useful for an identified channel or content purpose?

If any identity question fails, reject or correct the image before considering aesthetic approval.

# Detailed Rules

Identity protection:

• Nayara's face must remain consistent across all scenes.
• Never replace Nayara's identity with the face of an inspiration model.
• Never average multiple reference faces into a new person.
• Never alter ethnicity.
• Never significantly age or de age Nayara.
• Never reshape the face to follow beauty trends.
• Never enlarge the eyes, narrow the nose, over define the jaw, inflate the lips, or create artificial facial symmetry.
• Natural human asymmetry must remain acceptable.
• Skin must retain natural texture, tonal variation, and realistic pores.
• Teeth must remain natural in shape, scale, spacing, and brightness.
• Facial expressions may change, but the underlying face must not.

Current identity anchors:

These anchors remain active until the dedicated Face_Identity.md is approved:

• Brazilian woman in her early thirties.
• Medium warm skin tone with golden undertones.
• Oval face with soft features.
• Soft cheekbones and a defined but not exaggerated jawline.
• Dark brown almond shaped expressive eyes.
• Full defined brows with a natural arch.
• Bright natural smile.
• Dark brown to natural black hair.
• Long hair, straight to softly wavy.
• Thick and healthy texture without excessive volume.
• Polished, confident, warm, intelligent, and approachable overall presence.

Hair:

• Hair colour must remain dark brown to natural black.
• No blonde hair.
• No red hair.
• No copper hair.
• No visible caramel, blonde, or fashion highlights.
• No colour gradient that makes the hair appear lighter than the approved range.
• Hair may be straight or softly wavy.
• Hair must remain long unless a future approved identity document authorises another length.
• Volume must remain realistic and balanced.
• Do not create excessive volume on either side of the face.
• Hair must not obscure important facial features.
• A low bun or controlled tied style may be used when the scene requires it, provided the hairline and face remain accurate.

Skin and retouching:

• Preserve warm skin tone.
• Do not lighten the skin to match an inspiration model.
• Do not cool the skin into grey, pink, or desaturated tones.
• Do not over smooth the skin.
• Do not remove all natural lines, pores, or tonal variation.
• Do not add excessive glow, blur, contour, or artificial highlight.
• Retouching must resemble professional photography, not beauty filtering.

Makeup:

• Makeup must remain polished, warm, and natural.
• Brows must remain defined but believable.
• Eye makeup may enhance the eyes without changing their shape.
• Lip colour should remain warm nude, soft rose, or a comparable natural tone.
• No bright lipstick as a default.
• No heavy glamour makeup.
• No exaggerated contouring.
• No editorial makeup that competes with identity.

Wardrobe palette:

Approved core colours include:

• Soft white, approximately #F8F7F3.
• Ivory, approximately #F5F1E8.
• Warm cream, approximately #F2E8DC.
• Pearl, approximately #F1F0EB.
• Stone, approximately #D8CEC2.
• Light taupe, approximately #C9B8A6.
• Warm beige, approximately #D9C6AE.
• Oatmeal, approximately #D8CCBE.
• Camel, approximately #C69C6D.
• Mushroom, approximately #A79787.
• Chocolate brown, approximately #4A3428.
• Espresso, approximately #2D221D, for leather accessories.

Black rule:

• Black is reserved for dresses.
• Do not use black blazers.
• Do not use black shirts.
• Do not use black knitwear.
• Do not use black trousers.
• Do not use black jackets.
• Do not use black coats.
• Do not use black turtlenecks.
• Do not copy black separates from an inspiration image.
• When a reference uses black separates, preserve the pose or composition and replace the outfit with approved neutrals.

A black separate shown in a previously generated image is not automatically approved. Historical output does not override this rule.

Wardrobe structure:

• Prefer tailored clothing.
• Prefer defined shoulders.
• Prefer clean necklines.
• Prefer structured but natural silhouettes.
• Prefer matte or softly textured premium fabrics.
• Avoid busy patterns.
• Avoid visible logos.
• Avoid trend led cuts that will date quickly.
• Avoid outfits that feel like influencer styling.
• Avoid clothing that distracts from Nayara's face and message.

Jewellery:

Approved metals only:

• Yellow gold.
• Champagne gold.
• Brushed gold.

Permanent restrictions:

• No mixed gold and silver styling.
• No bright silver.
• No cool platinum.
• No oversized statement jewellery.
• No excessive rings.
• No visible jewellery that becomes the focal point.

Preferred jewellery:

• Small gold studs or small gold drop earrings.
• Small or controlled gold hoops where the scene supports them.
• Delicate gold necklace.
• Minimal gold rings.
• Thin gold bracelet when appropriate.

Watch:

• The watch must be gold.
• Do not generate a silver watch.
• Do not generate a mixed metal watch.
• Do not generate a dark strap as the default.
• Prefer a small, refined, rectangular gold watch inspired by the proportions of a Cartier Santos or comparable timeless design.
• The watch must remain correctly formed and proportionate.
• If the watch is visible, inspect the case, bracelet, face, hands, and wrist connection for artefacts.

Photography style:

• Photorealistic only for Photography OS outputs.
• Do not convert approved photography assets into illustration within this subsystem.
• Use natural skin texture.
• Use soft dimensional lighting.
• Use modern editorial composition.
• Use clean studio environments as the primary direction.
• Avoid obvious AI polish.
• Avoid plastic texture.
• Avoid over sharpened eyes and teeth.
• Avoid artificial depth of field around hair and hands.
• Avoid warped furniture, props, fingers, jewellery, and fabric.

Brand feeling:

Every image must prioritise:

• Authority before fashion.
• Warmth before drama.
• Modernity before trend.
• Consistency before novelty.
• Believability before spectacle.
• Substance before status.
• Proximity without loss of credibility.

Every image must avoid:

• Corporate stock feeling.
• Luxury influencer feeling.
• Fashion campaign feeling.
• Generic businesswoman identity.
• Cold executive posing.
• Performative busyness.
• Chaotic multitasking imagery.
• Overly glamorous presentation.
• Sexualised posing.
• Childish visual treatment.

Studio and background:

• Prefer soft white, cream, stone, warm greige, taupe, mushroom, or carefully controlled darker neutral backgrounds.
• Background colour must create adequate separation from hair and clothing.
• Backgrounds must remain simple.
• Avoid cluttered sets.
• Avoid obvious office stock environments as the default.
• Avoid colourful backgrounds that introduce an unapproved palette.
• Preserve negative space where the content format requires text.

Pose and expression:

• Body language must feel confident and natural.
• Posture may be relaxed but not collapsed.
• Hands must have a clear purpose.
• Avoid clenched, distorted, hidden, or anatomically incorrect hands.
• Direct eye contact may be used for authority.
• Side glances may be used for reflection, curiosity, or questioning content.
• Smiles must look genuine and consistent with Nayara.
• Avoid exaggerated surprise.
• Avoid intense fashion expressions.
• Avoid seductive posing.
• Avoid expressions that make Nayara appear unrecognisable.

Text inside images:

• Exact final text should normally be added after image generation.
• Use a controlled design tool for spelling, punctuation, font, hierarchy, and placement.
• Generated text must not be treated as final unless every character is verified.
• Text must not cover Nayara's face.
• Text must remain legible on mobile.
• Handwritten identity labels should normally be limited to five to seven.
• Typography must support authority and proximity rather than make the image feel childish.

Editing rule:

When Nayara requests one correction, preserve every approved element that was not requested to change.

Examples:

If the watch colour is wrong, correct the watch without redesigning the face, outfit, pose, background, or composition.

If hair volume is excessive on one side, reduce only that volume while preserving colour, face, expression, outfit, prop, and text.

If the face is inaccurate, fix the face while keeping the rest untouched when technically possible.

Asset approval:

An image is not approved because it was generated.

An image becomes approved only after Nayara explicitly accepts it or it passes the documented approval workflow once that workflow is approved.

Rejected and superseded images must not be placed in the approved portfolio.

Edge cases:

A reference may include a strong pose with an unapproved outfit. Keep the pose and restyle the outfit.

A black and white treatment may be used even though the source colours are not visible, but the original wardrobe styling must still follow the rules.

A content concept may require a printed sign or newspaper. The sign may use black typography. The black rule applies to clothing, not necessary graphic text or neutral props.

A chrome chair or silver studio equipment may appear as a prop. The metal rule applies to personal jewellery and watch styling, not all environmental objects.

# Good Examples

Good example:

Use the seated pose from a model wearing a black blazer, but dress Nayara in a stone blazer, cream top, and stone trousers with a gold watch.

Why this works:

The pose is borrowed while the brand wardrobe remains protected.

Good example:

Generate a warm greige studio portrait with dark brown to black hair, natural skin texture, a small gold watch, and a confident closed mouth smile.

Why this works:

It follows identity, wardrobe, metal, studio, and expression rules.

Good example:

Correct excessive hair volume on one side without changing the sign, chair, clothing, pose, or facial expression.

Why this works:

It follows the single correction editing rule.

# Bad Examples

Bad example:

Use a blonde inspiration model and retain lighter highlights because the pose looks good.

Why this fails:

The inspiration changes Nayara's identity.

Bad example:

Use black trousers with a cream blazer because black looks professional.

Why this fails:

It directly violates the permanent black rule.

Bad example:

Approve a silver or mixed metal watch because it is small.

Why this fails:

The watch rule is explicit and size does not remove the conflict.

Bad example:

Regenerate the entire image to fix one accessory and accidentally change the face.

Why this fails:

The correction creates new identity drift.

# Common Mistakes

• Treating visual similarity as identity accuracy.
• Allowing lighter hair because the lighting is warm.
• Approving black separates out of habit.
• Forgetting that the watch must be fully gold.
• Using mixed metals because they appear subtle.
• Removing all skin texture.
• Making the smile too large or too perfect.
• Increasing hair volume to make an image feel premium.
• Copying makeup from the inspiration model.
• Changing multiple elements during a targeted correction.
• Treating a generated output as approved by default.

# Anti Patterns

• Face drift.
• Ethnicity drift.
• Hair colour drift.
• Wardrobe exceptions without documentation.
• Silver accessory leakage.
• Plastic skin.
• Artificial teeth.
• Fashion model posing.
• Generic executive imagery.
• Uncontrolled regeneration.
• Approval by attractiveness.
• Inspiration treated as identity.

# Quality Checklist

Before approving a photograph, confirm:

• The face looks like Nayara.
• The skin tone remains warm and accurate.
• The face has not been reshaped or idealised.
• Hair is dark brown to natural black.
• Hair has no unapproved highlights.
• Hair volume is realistic.
• Clothing uses the approved palette.
• Black appears only in an approved dress, not separates.
• Jewellery uses approved gold tones only.
• The watch is fully gold.
• Makeup is polished and natural.
• Skin texture remains believable.
• Teeth and eyes remain realistic.
• Hands and accessories are anatomically correct.
• The image communicates authority and proximity.
• The result looks like a professional photograph.
• Only requested elements changed during correction.
• The image has a clear intended use.
• Approval status is explicit.
• The document follows the Nayara Brand OS Repository Standards.
