# Purpose

This document defines the visual character that every approved photograph of Nayara Fernandes must express.

It describes how the photography should feel, how authority and proximity should be balanced, and which visual signals belong or do not belong to the brand.

This document does not replace physical identity rules. It defines the emotional and aesthetic DNA around the identity.

# How this document connects to the rest of the repository

Photography_Philosophy.md defines why the photography exists.

Visual_DNA.md defines how that philosophy should look and feel.

Non_Negotiable_Rules.md defines what can never be compromised.

Future identity, wardrobe, studio, pose, scene, and workflow documents must use this Visual DNA as their creative filter.

When external inspiration conflicts with this document, adapt or reject the inspiration.

# Core Principles

Every approved photograph should feel:

• Modern.
• Editorial.
• Premium without excess.
• Human.
• Natural.
• Thoughtful.
• Confident.
• Structured.
• Calm.
• Warm.
• Intelligent.
• Believable.
• Useful for content.

Every approved photograph should avoid feeling:

• Influencer led.
• Luxury influencer led.
• Fashion campaign led.
• Corporate stock.
• Artificial.
• Over retouched.
• Cold.
• Overly serious.
• Trend dependent.
• Generic.
• Chaotic.
• Performatively busy.

# Decision Framework

Use the following visual scales as the default target:

Authority: 5 out of 5.

Warmth: 4 out of 5.

Editorial quality: 5 out of 5.

Corporate feeling: 2 out of 5.

Luxury signalling: 2 out of 5.

Lifestyle feeling: 3 out of 5.

Approachability: 4 out of 5.

Visual complexity: 2 out of 5.

When reviewing a photograph, ask:

1. Does Nayara look credible before she looks fashionable?
2. Does the image feel warm without becoming casual?
3. Does the image feel premium without appearing status driven?
4. Does it look editorial rather than corporate stock?
5. Does it look like a real photograph rather than a polished AI render?
6. Does the image support text, cropping, or design reuse?
7. Does the image belong beside the approved portfolio?
8. Does the photograph make Nayara recognisable before the outfit or background?

# Detailed Rules

First impression:

Confident, credible, composed.

Second impression:

Warm, approachable, intelligent.

Third impression:

Modern, ambitious, organised, capable of handling complexity.

Visual positioning statement:

Nayara should look like a modern customer success and technology professional who can explain difficult ideas clearly, manage competing priorities, build trust, and remain human while doing it.

Authority signals:

• Tailored silhouettes.
• Clean lines.
• Intentional posture.
• Controlled studio lighting.
• Clear eye direction.
• Calm facial muscles.
• Minimal distractions.
• Strong framing.
• High quality natural finish.
• Deliberate use of negative space.

Proximity signals:

• Genuine smiles.
• Thoughtful expressions.
• Natural hand positions.
• Slight body movement.
• Seated poses.
• Relaxed but upright posture.
• Personal annotations or handwritten labels added during design.
• Human props used sparingly.
• Expressions that suggest recognition, curiosity, humour, or reflection.

Premium signals:

• Precise tailoring.
• Quality fabric texture.
• Gold jewellery.
• A small gold watch.
• Warm neutral backgrounds.
• Soft dimensional lighting.
• Clean colour grading.
• Controlled composition.

Premium must never be communicated through excessive logos, oversized jewellery, luxury handbags, dramatic status props, or conspicuous wealth.

Studio direction:

The preferred studio environment is minimal and warm.

Approved background families include:

• Soft white.
• Warm cream.
• Light stone.
• Warm greige.
• Light taupe.
• Mushroom.
• Controlled charcoal only when the outfit and scene preserve warmth.

A preferred warm greige reference is approximately #C7C1B8.

Nearby acceptable variations include:

• #D0CBC5 for lighter wall areas.
• #BFB6AA for warmer shadow areas.
• #A8A59F for deeper controlled shadows.

Backgrounds should support separation from Nayara's hair, skin, and clothing. They must not become the focal point.

Lighting direction:

• Soft but dimensional.
• Warm neutral rather than orange.
• Flattering without flattening facial structure.
• Natural skin texture retained.
• Gentle contrast around cheekbones, jaw, eyes, and hair.
• No harsh beauty lighting.
• No excessive glow.
• No plastic skin finish.

Composition direction:

• Clean studio portraiture.
• Editorial framing.
• Vertical first for social media flexibility.
• Enough negative space for post text when required.
• Crops should preserve hands when hands carry meaning.
• Avoid cutting through joints.
• Keep the face large enough to remain recognisable on mobile.
• Props should be simple and purposeful.

Expression direction:

Approved expression families include:

• Warm direct smile.
• Closed mouth confident smile.
• Thoughtful side glance.
• Curious raised brow.
• Calm direct eye contact.
• Natural laugh.
• Focused working expression.
• Slightly questioning expression for contrarian content.
• Quiet reflective expression.

Expressions must remain believable for Nayara. Avoid exaggerated surprise, forced seriousness, intense fashion expressions, or overly seductive posing.

Text treatment direction:

For introduction, proximity, and multitasking content, handwritten notes may be placed around Nayara.

The style should be:

• White or approved high contrast text.
• Rounded handwritten marker style.
• Friendly and slightly irregular.
• Medium to bold weight.
• Clear on mobile.
• Informal but not childish.
• Positioned around the subject without covering the face.
• Slightly angled when useful.

Suitable font directions include Gloria Hallelujah, Patrick Hand, Comic Neue Bold, Gaegu Bold, More Sugar, or a comparable clean handwritten marker font.

Use five to seven labels as the default maximum.

Final text should normally be added in Canva, Figma, Photoshop, or another controlled design tool rather than trusted to image generation.

Colour direction:

The photographic wardrobe and set should be dominated by:

• Soft white.
• Ivory.
• Warm cream.
• Stone.
• Light taupe.
• Camel.
• Mushroom.
• Chocolate brown.
• Espresso for leather accessories.

Black is reserved for dresses under the permanent wardrobe rule.

The system must not introduce a general accent colour wardrobe merely because a colour is flattering.

Metal direction:

• Yellow gold.
• Champagne gold.
• Brushed gold.

No mixed metal styling.

No bright silver.

No cool platinum.

Edge cases:

A black and white photograph may be approved for a deliberate editorial use if the original identity remains accurate and the full portfolio does not become visually fragmented.

A darker background may be approved for a black dress portrait if hair separation, skin warmth, and facial detail remain clear.

A more playful composition may be approved for a social introduction or multitasking post if tailoring, identity, and visual quality remain controlled.

# Good Examples

Good example:

A warm greige studio portrait with Nayara in an ivory blazer, gold jewellery, direct eye contact, and a slight smile.

Why this works:

It creates authority, warmth, and clean reuse potential.

Good example:

A seated stone suit portrait with a thoughtful side glance and soft directional shadow.

Why this works:

It feels editorial and intelligent without becoming cold.

Good example:

A clean portrait surrounded by five handwritten notes about customer success, AI workflows, multitasking, Brazil, and Dublin.

Why this works:

It adds personal proximity without weakening the structured visual identity.

# Bad Examples

Bad example:

A glossy luxury set with a designer handbag, strong jewellery, and dramatic posing.

Why this fails:

Status becomes the visual message instead of expertise and trust.

Bad example:

A bright office stock image with generic smiling colleagues.

Why this fails:

It removes distinctiveness and looks commercially staged.

Bad example:

A heavily retouched portrait with enlarged eyes, perfectly smooth skin, and excessive hair volume.

Why this fails:

It weakens identity accuracy and believability.

# Common Mistakes

• Using beige as a substitute for visual strategy.
• Making every background the same tone without checking subject separation.
• Creating premium feeling through visible luxury products.
• Making authority look stern.
• Making proximity look casual.
• Using handwritten text that feels childish.
• Adding too many labels around the subject.
• Allowing text to compete with the face.
• Using hair volume or glamour lighting to create impact.
• Treating black as a default professional colour.

# Anti Patterns

• Beige influencer aesthetic.
• Quiet luxury costume styling.
• Generic female founder imagery.
• Boss woman visual clichés.
• Excessive retouching.
• Heavy contouring.
• Trend colour dependency.
• Uncontrolled handwritten doodles.
• Dramatic editorial coldness.
• Flat corporate lighting.
• Props that signal status instead of substance.

# Quality Checklist

Before approving a photograph, confirm:

• It feels modern, editorial, premium, human, and believable.
• Authority is strong.
• Warmth and approachability remain present.
• The image does not feel like corporate stock.
• The image does not feel like an influencer campaign.
• Luxury signalling is restrained.
• The background supports the subject.
• Lighting preserves natural facial dimension.
• The face remains the primary focus.
• Text placement, when used, supports rather than covers the subject.
• The image works on mobile and within social media layouts.
• The image belongs beside the approved portfolio.
• The document follows the Nayara Brand OS Repository Standards.
