# Purpose

This document defines how to rewrite weak hooks into sharper, clearer, more brand aligned hooks. It is used when the first version is too generic, too dramatic, too vague, or too disconnected from the post.

# How this document connects to the rest of the repository

Re_Hook_Library.md depends on Hook_Library.md. It should be used during revision, review, and post optimisation.

# Core Principles

• Rewriting a hook should sharpen the idea, not decorate it.
• The revised hook must be more specific than the original.
• The revised hook must match the post body.
• The revised hook must protect the brand voice.
• The revised hook should improve clarity before cleverness.

# Decision Framework

When rewriting a hook, ask:

1. What is the real tension?
2. Who is the reader?
3. What is too vague in the current hook?
4. What business consequence is missing?
5. What wording sounds too generic or dramatic?
6. Can the hook be shorter?

# Detailed Rules

Rewrite patterns:

Broad to specific:

Replace general statements with a concrete audience, risk, or outcome.

Trend to consequence:

Replace trend language with what the trend changes for people or business.

Soft to sharper:

Replace vague positivity with a clearer point of view.

Product to problem:

Replace platform language with the decision the reader needs to make.

Rules:

Preserve the core idea.

Remove filler.

Avoid fake drama.

Keep the first line readable on mobile.

Edge cases:

If the weak hook is personal, keep the emotion but sharpen the lesson.

If the weak hook is technical, simplify the consequence.

If the weak hook is too aggressive, reduce drama without losing tension.

# Good Examples

Weak:

AI is changing how companies work.

Better:

AI does not expose a technology gap first. It exposes a capability gap.

Why this works:

It moves from generic trend to brand specific reframe.

Weak:

Upskilling matters more than ever.

Better:

Upskilling only matters when it changes what the business can actually execute.

Why this works:

It connects learning to execution.

# Bad Examples

Bad rewrite:

This one AI mistake could destroy your business.

Why this fails:

It creates exaggerated fear and damages trust.

Bad rewrite:

Learning is super important for everyone.

Why this fails:

It stays broad and weak.

# Common Mistakes

• Making the hook louder instead of clearer.
• Rewriting without understanding the post.
• Adding drama to create attention.
• Keeping vague words from the original.
• Removing the business consequence.

# Anti Patterns

• Clickbait rewrites.
• Cosmetic rewrites.
• Generic motivational rewrites.
• Hooks that shift the post topic.
• Hooks that sound like a vendor campaign.
